Review

In Manjummel Boys, Chidambaram ensures that every detail serves a purpose, from a character’s loud screams to a seemingly casual tug-of-war both returning with satisfying payoffs. Building on his debut Jan-E-Man, where he skillfully balanced humor and morbidity, Chidambaram takes on a new challenge here, keeping the audience engaged despite them knowing the outcome, as the film is inspired by real events.

The story starts light-heartedly, with the gang crashing a wedding and clashing with rivals, before taking a sharp turn when one friend falls into a ravine. By then, the audience is familiar with the distinct traits of the eleven friends, making the emotional stakes stronger. While the survival thriller recalls Bharathan’s Malootty, Manjummel Boys focuses more on friendship. A pivotal moment cuts from the crisis to their childhood, showing how past bonds and instincts shape their present actions, adding emotional depth to the thriller.
