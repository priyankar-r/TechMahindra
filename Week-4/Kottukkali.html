Review

Kottukkaali is director PS Vinothraj’s powerful sophomore feature, surpassing his acclaimed debut Koozhangal. Like his first film, Kottukkaali explores family dynamics, patriarchy, and caste pride through a journey narrative. The film centers on Meena (a stellar Anna Ben), whose family believes she’s under the spell of her unseen lover from an oppressed caste. They force her into rituals to "cure" her, while Pandi (a fiery Soori), her betrothed, seethes with rage.

Vinothraj’s masterful storytelling blends metaphors from nature — a red rooster mirroring Meena’s desire for freedom, and a stubborn bull blocking the path, eventually moved by kindness — to highlight themes of control, repression, and resistance. The film critiques generational patriarchy and caste arrogance, while also showing how women, though often silenced, can both challenge and uphold regressive beliefs. The film’s strength lies in its subtlety: minimal dialogue, naturalistic camerawork, and the absence of background music heighten the raw emotions. Anna Ben’s near-silent performance as Meena is deeply moving, while Soori delivers his career-best as the volatile Pandi. The supporting cast enriches the narrative, with moments of humor cleverly undercutting toxic masculinity.
