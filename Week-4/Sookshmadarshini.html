Review

Sookshmadarshini is a cleverly crafted thriller that turns a seemingly simple neighborhood story into an engaging mystery. Priyadarshini (Nazriya Nazim), a nosy microbiology graduate, shifts her microscope-like curiosity from microbes to her new neighbor, Manuel (Basil Joseph). Though Manuel appears kind and caring, there’s something subtly off about him—something only Priyadarshini picks up on.

The narrative thrives on this suspicion, with Priyadarshini and her bumbling detective friends piecing together clues, often connected through WhatsApp. The screenplay, by Libin TB and Athul Ramachandran, keeps the audience guessing with smart diversions, unexpected humor, and a rewarding payoff that ties up even the smallest details. Basil Joseph steps out of his usual cheerful roles to explore a more mysterious side, while Nazriya’s vibrant comeback adds charm, though occasionally overplayed. Sookshmadarshini is an intriguing watch that blends suspense and humor, cementing Malayalam cinema’s reputation for fresh and quality storytelling.
